The Drop

Three SKUs. Six weeks. One project.

Éclat Studio had a concept — The Lemon Edit, a limited three-product drop built on their Soft Glow base formula. They had a team, a manufacturer, and six weeks. What they didn't have was one place to run it.

The brief was in Notion, assets were in Drive, copy was in email threads, and the ops contractor was asking Slack questions that had already been answered somewhere else. Nobody had a real picture of where things actually stood.

They set up a single Trak project and ran the entire launch from it — brief to sold out.

For the person running the launch

Full visibility. No status meetings.

Managing a launch means knowing exactly where things stand at any moment — across SKUs, across your team, across every part of the project. Trak gives you that picture without having to ask anyone for it.

Before Trak, Nour was running status updates manually — a Friday Slack message, a shared Notion table she had to update herself, and a running note on her phone of what she was afraid people were forgetting.

With Trak, she opened the Overview tab and knew. Every task across every SKU, every assignee, every status — pulled into one view automatically. Nothing to maintain. Nothing to ask.

And when she wanted a breakdown by tab or assignee, she didn't build a pivot table. She asked the AI. In fifteen seconds, she had a live chart sitting inside the project — not a screenshot, not a doc. A chart that updated as work got done.
