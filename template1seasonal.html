The Drop

Three SKUs. Six weeks. One project.

Éclat Studio had a concept — The Lemon Edit, a limited three-product drop built on their Soft Glow base formula. They had a team, a manufacturer, and six weeks. What they didn't have was one place to run it. The brief was in Notion, assets were in Drive, copy was in email threads, and the ops contractor was asking Slack questions that had already been answered somewhere else.

They set up a single Trak project and ran the entire launch from it — brief to brief, shoot to sold out.

The Result

950 units. 47 minutes. One project.

The Lemon Edit went live at 9:00am on July 14. The email went out at 9:05. By 9:47, every unit across all three SKUs was gone. $28,850 in revenue. 73% of first-hour orders came from the email list. TikTok drove the rest — the mid-spray mist shot, shared by two creators from the seeding list, accounted for 40% of TikTok traffic.

47 min
Full sellout — all 950 units

Email list drove 73% of first-hour orders. TikTok drove the rest. No paid ads. No discount codes. The product and the list did the work.

Waitlist signups in 48 hours

"We've done launches where half the team didn't know the brief had changed. This time everyone was working from the same project, the same tabs, the same assets. Launch morning was the calmest it's ever been."

The waitlist collected 1,240 emails in the 48 hours after sellout. The restock decision — how many moisturizer units, whether to make The Lemon Edit permanent — was made inside the Post-Launch tab, next to the chart that showed the moisturizer had driven 36% of revenue on 26% of inventory. The answer was obvious. The team started the next drop brief on August 1.
