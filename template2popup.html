The Situation

A great space, a short window, and a lot to coordinate.

Maison Cèdre makes elevated basics — structured linen trousers, heavyweight cotton tees, and a cedar-dyed overshirt that had sold out twice. Their customers kept asking where they could try things on, so when a Greene Street space opened up for four days in October, creative director Léa said yes.

Six weeks out, the planning started. A pop-up has a different kind of operational load than an online launch — there's a physical space to design, vendors to coordinate, permits to pull, a merchandise plan to finalize, and staff to schedule. The team was Léa on creative, ops lead Sana on logistics, a PR contact, and two part-time floor staff.

They set up a Trak project with four tabs and worked from it until doors opened on October 9.

The Build

Six weeks. Four tabs. One project.

Sana ran the vendor and logistics side out of the Space & Logistics tab — every quote, deposit status, and permit in a single table she could update as things moved. Léa kept the creative direction and merch plan in Creative & Styling. Event HQ was the shared view they both referenced. Day-Of got built out two weeks before the event and was the only thing open on Sana's phone on October 9.

The Result

Four days. $41,200. Onto the next one.

The pop-up ran October 9–12. 340 units, $41,200 in revenue. The Cedar Overshirt was the standout — 46% of total revenue, nearly sold out by day three. The Day-Of tab stayed open on Sana's phone for the duration.

Léa was back in Paris by October 13. The post-event notes were already in Event HQ. The next project brief started the following week.
