How it works
Trak gives you three ways to work across projects: Workflow pages — an open-ended AI you talk to, that knows everything in your workspace and can build, update, and analyze anything; the Everything Table — every item across all projects, filterable any way you want; and on-the-fly charts and boards built from whatever combination of project data you need.
Workflow pages

An AI that knows your entire workspace — ask it anything, tell it to do anything

A Workflow page is a canvas with an AI panel on the side. It's not a chatbot with preset commands — it's an open-ended analyst and operator that has full context of everything in your workspace.

🔎
Ask any question across any project
Ask "what's behind across all active projects right now?" or "which project has the most open urgent tasks?" or "compare the timeline on Summer Drop vs Brand Refresh" — and get a real answer drawn from your live workspace data, not a template.
⚡
Tell it to build what you need
It's not just answers — the AI can take action. Ask it to create a table of all overdue tasks across every project, generate a status chart, build a tracker, or draft a new block layout. Whatever you can do in the UI, it can do from a message.
✏️
Update information without digging through tabs
Tell it to mark a task complete, reassign something, update a project status, or change a priority — across any project — without navigating there yourself. One instruction, done.
This is the difference between a search tool and an operator. You're not looking things up — you're talking to something that understands the full picture and can act on it.

The Everything Table

Every asset across every project — filtered down to exactly what you want

The Everything Table is its own page in Trak — every task, file, block, and item from every project in one place. The point isn't to show you everything at once. It's to let you slice it until you're looking at only what matters.

🗂️
Filter by any combination of conditions
Filter by project, type, status, assignee, priority, due date, or tag — stack as many filters as you need. Show only urgent tasks assigned to the Creative team across the two projects launching this month. Two clicks.
📂
See assets from multiple projects side by side
Running two campaigns at once? Filter to show assets from both projects together. Compare what's in flight, what's overdue, and who owns what — without switching between project tabs.
↗️
Click through directly to the item in context
Every row in the Everything Table links back to where it lives — its tab, its block, its project. The table is a navigation layer. You're always one click from the actual work.

On-the-fly charts and boards

Build any view across any projects — from a single prompt

Tell Trak's AI what you want to see across your projects and it generates it as a chart, board, or table — immediately, from your real data. Not a template. Not a preset. Exactly what you asked for.

📊
Cross-project charts from a single prompt
Ask for a bar chart of open tasks across all projects, a breakdown of work by status across your Q2 launches, a timeline comparing deadlines across three projects — and it's built on the spot. No configuration, no data export.
🔀
Combine any data, any way you want
"Show me everything due in the next two weeks across Summer Drop and Brand Refresh, grouped by assignee." The conditions are yours. You're not limited to what a dashboard widget was designed to show — you're describing exactly what you need to know.
📌
Keep it or throw it away
Generated views drop in as blocks on your Workflow canvas. Pin the ones that are useful to your dashboard for a persistent cross-project view, or use them once for a meeting and move on. No cleanup required.
There's no "reporting" feature to configure. The AI is the reporting feature. You describe what you want to see and it builds it. The only limit is how specific you can be with your question.
