What's covered
Trak's internal layer has three parts: Internal spaces — projects built exactly like any other project, but scoped to your team's ops work; Docs — a dedicated rich-text editor for SOPs, guides, and resources, with block references and images; and Clients — a database of every external partner and brand relationship, each with their own detail page.
Internal spaces

A dedicated space for how your team operates — built like any other project

Internal spaces are projects scoped to your team's own operations rather than an external campaign. They work exactly the same way — tabs, blocks, tasks, timelines, files — just pointed inward.

📁
Organize ops work the same way you organize everything else
Create an internal space for Onboarding, one for Brand Guidelines, one for Seasonal Planning — each with its own tabs and blocks. The same structure you use for active projects works just as well for how your team runs itself.
✅
Tasks, timelines, and files — the full block system
Internal spaces have access to every block type. Add a task list for recurring housekeeping, a timeline for quarterly planning cycles, a file block for brand assets, a gallery for approved visual references. Anything you'd put in a project, you can put here.
🔒
Visible only to your team
Internal spaces are exactly that — internal. They're never exposed externally and don't show up in Magic Link views. Your SOPs and internal planning stay separate from what anyone outside your team ever sees.
One fewer tool. Most brand ops teams keep their internal processes in a separate Notion or Confluence. In Trak, your ops infrastructure lives in the same workspace as your active work — same interface, same search, same AI.

Docs

A dedicated doc editor for everything your team needs to write down and reference

Docs are separate from project tabs — they have their own dedicated rich-text editor, built for the kind of content that lives outside of tasks and blocks. SOPs, brand guidelines, onboarding guides, process write-ups.

✍️
Rich text that actually feels like a doc
Headings, body text, bold, lists, images — a proper writing environment, not a notes field. Write your launch SOP the same way you'd write it anywhere, and it stays readable and well-structured for whoever references it later.
🔗
Mention blocks directly inside a doc
Reference a specific block — a task list, a timeline, an asset — directly inside your doc text. Your launch SOP can mention the @Shoot Checklist block or the @Shopify Setup tab so that the written process and the actual work are always one click apart.
🖼️
Add images inline
Drop images directly into the doc — annotated screenshots, visual references, approved mockups. Useful for anything that needs a visual to make sense: brand guidelines, packaging specs, layout references.
Docs and projects stay connected. A doc can live inside an internal space or alongside a project. Either way, it's in the same workspace — findable by AI search, accessible to your team, and never buried in a Drive folder nobody opens.

Clients

Every external relationship in one place — with their own page in Trak

Clients in Trak aren't just a name attached to a project. Each client has their own dedicated page — a single place to hold everything about that relationship.

🗃️
A database of every external actor
Everyone you work with externally lives in the Clients section — agencies, retail partners, PR contacts, brand collaborators. Browse them as a list, search by name or company, and open any record in seconds.
📋
Each client has their own detail page
Open any client and you get their full record — contact details, company, notes, and every project they're linked to. Add context that doesn't fit anywhere else: how they prefer to communicate, what they've approved, what's still outstanding.
↗️
Linked to every project they're part of
When you attach a client to a project, that project shows up on their page. You can see every active and past engagement for that client from one place — no searching, no cross-referencing.
Connected to Magic Links. Once a client exists in Trak, sharing a project view with them is one step — generate a Magic Link, choose what they see, and send it. Their feedback comes back into the same record.
