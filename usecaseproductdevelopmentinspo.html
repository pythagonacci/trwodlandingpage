How to run product development in Trak

A step-by-step walkthrough of how a physical product team sets up and manages development — from first brief to launch-ready.

Start free Book a walkthrough
How it works
You create one project per product, connect it to Shopify, break it into tabs by phase, and fill each tab with the blocks that phase actually needs — briefs, tasks, files, timelines, Figma embeds. Everything your team needs to develop a product lives in that one project.
Step 1

Create a project and connect it to your Shopify product

1
Go to Projects and create a new project
Name it after the product. Set a status — In Progress — a due date, a priority, and tags like skincare or serum. These propagate to your dashboard and calendar automatically.
2
Attach it to the Shopify product
If the product already exists in your store — even as a draft — connect it from your product library. Trak pulls in the name, images, variant structure, and store metadata. You can also create the project directly from the product page and Trak does this automatically.
3
The Shopify block appears on your first tab
It shows live inventory, units sold, variant count, and price — so your ops context lives right next to your development work from the start.

Step 2

Create a tab for each phase of your process

1
Hit "New tab" and name it after a phase
Start with your first phase — Brief & Concept. Then add Formulation, Packaging & Design, Samples & Review, Launch Ready. Each tab is a separate canvas. Your formulator works in Formulation, your creative director in Packaging — same project, separate workspaces.
2
Add subtabs if a phase gets complex
Right-click any tab and select Add subtab. If Packaging needs its own sub-pages — Dieline and Mood Board, for example — nest them under it. The sidebar updates automatically so nothing gets buried.
3
Each tab starts blank — you build it with blocks
Click the + on any tab to add a block. Text, tasks, files, timelines, galleries, embeds — choose what that phase actually needs. The next few steps show you exactly what to add for product development.

Step 3

Add your files, Figma comps, and visual assets to the right tab

1
On Formulation — add a file block for lab documents
Click + → File block. Drag in your lab results, supplier specs, and formula briefs. You can attach multiple files to one block — round one results, round two, revised brief — all versioned in place. No Drive folder to maintain separately.
2
On Packaging & Design — embed your Figma comp
Click + → Embed block and paste the Figma link. The live frame renders inside the tab. Your team reviews the actual design without switching tools — and when your designer updates it in Figma, Trak reflects it automatically.
3
Add a gallery block for mood boards and visual references
Click + → Gallery block and upload your reference images. Texture inspiration, material swatches, competitive packaging — all visible in the canvas next to the brief they're informing.

Step 4

Add a task block to each tab to track phase-specific actions

1
Click + → Task block on each tab
On Formulation, add tasks for lab-related actions: submit brief, review stability results, approve revised formula, send to regulatory. On Packaging, add tasks for design and print steps. Set an owner, due date, and priority on each task inline.
2
Switch to board view to see status at a glance
Toggle the task block to board view from the block menu. Tasks sort into columns — Not Started, In Review, Done — automatically based on their status. No manual drag needed.
3
Check the project overview for the full picture
Click Overview in the tab bar. Every task across every tab surfaces here — due today, due this week, overdue — each linking back to the exact tab and block it lives in.

Step 5

Add a timeline block to map your development schedule

1
Click + → Timeline block
Add it to your project's main tab or a dedicated planning tab. Create a milestone for each phase — first lab submission, stability window, sample receipt, packaging approval, bulk production start.
2
Set dates and assign each milestone
Click a milestone to set a start and end date, then assign it to the right person — your ops lead on lab milestones, your creative director on packaging, your supplier contact on production. The timeline visualizes the full schedule across all phases.
3
It syncs to the workspace calendar automatically
Every milestone shows up in the calendar the moment you create it. When a date moves, the calendar reflects it. No manual update, no "just checking — did the timeline change?"

Step 6

Review designs and files by commenting directly on the block

1
Open the comment panel on any block
Hover over any block and click the comment icon in the toolbar. The comment panel opens alongside the block. Leave feedback pinned to exactly what you're looking at — a packaging mockup, a lab result PDF, a mood board image.
2
Use @ to pull a teammate into the thread
Type @name in any comment. They get a notification that links directly to the block — not a Slack message saying "can you check the packaging thing." They land in the right context immediately.
3
Thread and resolve comments as the work moves forward
Reply inline to create a thread. When feedback is addressed, resolve the comment. The full review history stays attached to the block — so two weeks later you can see exactly what was flagged, who responded, and what changed.

Step 7

Share specific tabs with suppliers or founders using a Magic Link

1
Turn on the client page from the project header
Click the share toggle in the project header. This enables the public client view. Nothing is visible yet — you choose exactly which tabs to expose in the next step.
2
Mark specific tabs as visible and set a client-facing title
Right-click any tab and select Make public. Set a client-facing title if the internal name — "Packaging & Design" — doesn't make sense for your supplier. They'll only see the tabs you've explicitly made public.
3
Copy the Magic Link and send it
No login required. They see a clean view of just those tabs — your brief, your Figma embed, your files. They leave comments directly on the page. Those comments appear in your project labeled as external feedback, separate from your internal thread.

Step 8

Use AI to build blocks, edit content, and search across your project

1
Hit ⌘K and describe what you need built
Type something like "set up a packaging review tab with a brief section, approval task list, and a two-week timeline." Trak creates the blocks on the canvas — a text block, a task block, a timeline block — ready to fill in. Not a suggestion. The blocks are there.
2
Select any block to edit it with AI
Click a block, open the AI panel, and ask it to rewrite — revise the formulation brief after a direction change, restructure a task list after a scope change, summarize a supplier PDF you've attached. Every AI edit is undoable from the block toolbar.
3
Use ⌘K to search the whole project conversationally
Ask "what was the feedback on round two samples?" — Trak finds the comment, on the block, in the tab. You don't have to remember where it lives or open each tab to check.

Step 9

Tag any block with a status — then pull a cross-project view instantly

1
Open the properties panel on any block
Click the properties icon on any block — a Figma embed, a file, a gallery, a task. Add a tag (like packaging or regulatory) and set a status (In Review, Approved, Needs Revision). This works on every block type, not just tasks.
2
Tag as you work — across every tab
Your designer tags a Figma comp on Packaging as packaging · in review. Another comp on Concept gets concept · approved. A third on Samples gets sampling · needs revision. Three different tabs. Same tagging system.
3
Ask AI to aggregate by tag or status
Hit ⌘K and ask "Show me all Figma files and their current status." Trak pulls every matching block from across the project into a board — grouped by status, with the source tab shown on each card. No separate tracker needed. The tags you set while working become the view you use to manage.
Works for anything. Every file tagged regulatory. Every task tagged supplier. Every asset tagged final. Ask for it, get a live board — built from the work already in the canvas.
