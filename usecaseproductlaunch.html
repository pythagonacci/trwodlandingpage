Product Drop & Launch

How to run a multi-SKU launch in Trak

A step-by-step walkthrough of how teams coordinate a new line drop — from Shopify setup to campaign execution to influencer sends — in one project.

Start free Book a walkthrough
How it works
You create one project for the entire launch — every product in the line connected from Shopify, every workstream organized into its own tab. Your ops lead, creative director, and marketing team all work in the same project, in the parts that belong to them, with one shared launch date keeping everything aligned.
Step 1

Create one project for the entire line launch

1
Go to Projects and create a new project
Name it after the collection — Summer Line Launch or Hydration Collection — Q2. Set the launch date as the due date. Tag it with the collection name so it's searchable across your workspace. Set priority to Urgent — this is your most time-sensitive project.
2
Add every product in the line from Shopify
Instead of one Shopify block, you'll add a Shopify product block for each SKU in the collection. Go to your Products page, find each product in the line, and add it to the project. Each one gets its own block showing live inventory, variant count, and status — so you can see the full line at a glance without leaving the project.
3
Pin your launch date visibly on the first tab
Add a text block at the top of the overview tab with the launch date, collection name, and any hard constraints — retailer deadlines, PR embargo dates, paid campaign go-live. This becomes the single reference your whole team checks when they're not sure what "on time" means for this drop.

Step 2

Structure your tabs around launch workstreams, not departments

1
Create a tab for each workstream in the launch
A new line launch typically runs across several parallel tracks. Create a tab for each one — Campaign & Creative, Shopify Setup, Influencer & PR, Copy & Messaging, Launch Day. Each runs independently but shares the same project, the same launch date, and the same overview.
2
Assign ownership at the tab level
Your creative lead owns Campaign & Creative. Your ops lead owns Shopify Setup. Your marketing lead owns Influencer & PR. They each work in their own tab — building their blocks, managing their tasks — without stepping on each other. Everyone still sees the same project overview.
3
Add a Launch Day tab as the final checklist
Create a dedicated Launch Day tab with a single task block — every action that needs to happen on drop day, assigned and time-stamped. Shopify collections go live at 9am. Email sends at 9:05. Stories go up at 9:15. This tab becomes the coordination layer when everyone is moving at once.

Step 3

Use the Shopify Setup tab to track every product's readiness for launch

1
Add a Shopify product block for each SKU on this tab
Pull in each product in the collection — Hydra-Burst Serum, Moisture Barrier Cream, Lip Balm, Hydra-Mist Toner. Each block shows its current Shopify status: Draft, Scheduled, or Active. You can see at a glance which products are ready and which still need work before the drop date.
2
Tag each product block with its readiness status
Use universal properties to tag each product block — copy ready, images uploaded, pricing confirmed, collection added. As your ops lead works through each SKU, they update the status. The whole team can see which products are launch-ready without asking.
3
Add a task block for Shopify-specific launch actions
Below the product blocks, add a task block for setup actions: create the collection page, add all SKUs to the drop collection, set inventory levels, schedule the Shopify launch time, test the checkout flow. Assign each to your ops lead with the day-before launch as the deadline.
On launch day, your ops lead refreshes the Shopify blocks and sees live inventory and status update in real time — right inside Trak, without leaving the project.

Step 4

Build the Campaign & Creative tab as your creative production hub

1
Add a text block for the campaign brief
Write the campaign direction at the top — theme, visual references, tone, hero message, channel breakdown. This is the brief your creative team, copywriter, and photographer are all working from. Having it in the tab means no one is working off a stale version emailed two weeks ago.
2
Add a gallery block for the campaign's visual direction
Upload your mood board, reference images, and approved campaign visuals as they come in. Tag each image block with its status — reference, approved, final. As your creative lead signs off on assets, the status updates and your whole team can see what's locked.
3
Embed the campaign Figma file
Use an embed block to pull in the Figma file with all channel-specific creative — email header, PDP banners, paid social, story templates. Your team reviews and comments on the live file in Trak. When your designer updates it in Figma, it's reflected instantly — no re-sharing links.
4
Add a timeline block for creative deadlines
Map every creative deadline — photography delivery, first design pass, revision window, final lock. Assign each milestone to the right person. The timeline is visible on the tab so your creative lead can see the full production schedule without opening a separate doc.

Step 5

Manage your influencer and PR sends from a single tab

1
Build a table block for your influencer list
Add a table block to the Influencer & PR tab. Each row is a contact — influencer name, handle, tier, products they're receiving, send date, tracking status. You can filter by tier or status inline, so your marketing lead can see at a glance who's been sent product and who's still pending.
2
Attach the PR brief and gifting notes as file blocks
Upload the PR brief, the gifting messaging guide, and the unboxing notes as file blocks next to the table. When your team is pulling together packages, everything they need — who's getting what, what to include in the note, which products to prioritize — is in one tab.
3
Use Magic Links to share send details with your PR partner
If you're working with an external PR agency or a freelance gifting coordinator, toggle the Influencer & PR tab as public and send a Magic Link. They see the send list, the brief, the timeline — nothing else in the project. They leave notes and updates directly in Trak.

Step 6

Add a master launch timeline that spans every workstream

1
Add a timeline block to the Overview tab
This is the master launch timeline — one view that shows every workstream's critical path. Creative production, Shopify readiness, influencer sends, campaign go-live. Each one as a bar on the same timeline, all anchored to the same May 14 launch date.
2
Assign each milestone to the right owner
Click each bar to assign it. Your creative lead owns the creative production track. Your ops lead owns Shopify setup. Your marketing lead owns influencer sends and campaign go-live. Ownership is visible on the timeline so there's no ambiguity about who's responsible when a milestone slips.
3
The timeline syncs to the workspace calendar
Every milestone appears in the shared calendar the moment it's created. When a deadline moves — creative delivery pushed by two days, influencer send delayed — update it in the timeline and the calendar reflects it across the whole team immediately.

Step 7

Review campaign assets and give feedback directly on the block

1
Open the comment panel on any creative block
When your designer uploads a campaign image or an email header, hover over the block and open the comment panel. Leave feedback pinned to exactly what you're looking at — the hero image crop, the font choice on the PDP banner, the color balance on the product shot. No Slack thread, no "which version did you mean."
2
Use @ to loop in whoever needs to weigh in
Type @name to tag your founder on the final campaign direction, your copywriter on a headline call, your ops lead on a product detail. They land directly on the block — not in a thread trying to figure out what you're referring to.
3
Resolve comments as assets get locked
When a round of feedback is addressed, resolve the comment thread. As launch day approaches, you can see at a glance which assets still have open feedback and which are fully signed off. Nothing accidentally ships with unresolved notes.

Step 8

Use AI to build launch infrastructure and search across every workstream

1
Generate a full tab setup with ⌘K
Hit ⌘K and ask Trak to set up a workstream tab — "Create a Launch Day tab with a timed task checklist for a 9am drop — Shopify, email, social, paid." It builds the task block with pre-populated items, times, and suggested owners. You fill in the specifics; Trak handles the structure.
2
Ask AI to draft copy blocks from your brief
With your campaign brief in the canvas, ask AI to generate a first draft of your collection page headline, your launch email subject line, or your Instagram caption set. It reads the brief in context and writes from it — not from scratch. Every draft is editable and undoable.
3
Search across all workstreams with a single prompt
When you're days out from launch and moving fast, use ⌘K to find anything instantly — "which influencers haven't been sent product yet", "what's the status of the email creative", "show me all overdue tasks across the project." Trak finds it across every tab.

Step 9

Tag assets across workstreams — then pull a launch-readiness view instantly

1
Tag every deliverable block with its SKU and status
As your team uploads assets and completes work across tabs, tag each block with the product it belongs to — hydra-serum, moisture-cream, lip-balm, hydra-toner — and a status: in progress, review, approved, final. A campaign image in Creative gets tagged. A copy doc in Messaging gets tagged. A Shopify block in Setup gets tagged.
2
Ask AI for a readiness view per SKU
Hit ⌘K and ask "Show me all assets for the Hydra-Burst Serum and their status." Trak pulls every tagged block across every tab — the campaign image, the copy doc, the PDP asset, the Shopify block — into a single board grouped by status. You see instantly what's done and what's still open for that product, without opening five tabs.
3
Run a full launch readiness check the day before
Ask "Show me everything tagged as pending or in review across the whole project." Every outstanding item — across Creative, Shopify Setup, Influencer, Copy — surfaces in one view. You know exactly what still needs to close before 9am tomorrow.
No status spreadsheet. No "can you send me a list of what's still outstanding." The tags you set while building the launch become your pre-launch audit — pulled on demand, always current.
