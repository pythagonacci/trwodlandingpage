What's covered
Three ways to find and understand your work: AI search — ask a question, get an answer with sources; Workflow pages — a canvas where you talk to Trak's AI and shape your data any way you need; and AI analysis — analyze anything in your workspace, from files to projects to your entire team's workload.
AI search

Ask about your work. Get an answer.

Trak reads everything in your workspace — tasks, docs, blocks, files, comments — and gives you a direct answer to whatever you ask. Not a list of results. An actual answer, with sources.

🏷️
Everything is already tagged and queryable
Priority, due date, assignee, and status live on everything in Trak — not just tasks. So you can ask "show me everything high priority and overdue" and get back tasks, timeline events, and blocks all at once. No filter setup, no custom view needed.
🔎
It reads meaning, not just keywords
Ask "what did we decide about Summer Drop packaging?" and Trak finds the right content even if those exact words don't appear anywhere. It understands what you're asking across your blocks, docs, comments, and files.
📎
Every answer cites its source
Answers link back to the block, doc, or file they came from — clickable, one tap away. You never have to wonder whether what Trak told you is still accurate.

Workflow pages

Your data, shaped however you need it

A Workflow page is a blank canvas with Trak's AI alongside it. Describe what you want to see and it builds it right there — a table, a chart, a breakdown, a summary. Keep pushing until it looks exactly right.

💬
Describe it, get it
Say "table of every overdue task by project" or "how is work spread across the team this week" — and it appears on the canvas. No filters to configure, no report builder to navigate.
🔀
Refine through conversation
"Break that down by priority." "Turn this into a chart." "Only urgent ones." The canvas updates as you go. You're shaping the output in real time, not starting over each time.
📌
Keep it or move on
Anything on the canvas is a real block — pin it to your dashboard, drop it into a project tab, or just use it once and close the page. Nothing stays unless you want it to.
It acts, not just answers. Tell Trak to update a task, reassign work, change a project status — from the same canvas. Understanding your work and acting on it happen in the same place.

AI analysis

Analyze anything — your projects, your team, your files

Trak's AI doesn't just find things. It reads and analyzes everything in your workspace — your project data, your team's workload, your uploaded files — and tells you what's actually going on.

📊
Analyze your workspace data
Ask Trak to analyze anything — how a project is progressing, where your team is stretched, which projects are at risk, what's fallen through the cracks. It reads across all your projects and gives you a real picture, not a raw data dump.
📄
Read what's inside your files
Upload a brief, a contract, a supplier spec, a rate card — and ask what's inside instead of reading through it yourself. Ask "what's the MOQ for debossed packaging?" and get the answer with a citation to the exact page. Works with PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets, and text files.
💾
Save what's useful
Any analysis becomes a block — drop it onto a project canvas, attach it as a comment, or open it in a Workflow page to keep going. Files you upload stay part of your workspace knowledge, so AI search can find them later too.
There's no limit to what you can analyze. If it's in your workspace — a comment thread, a project timeline, a doc, a spreadsheet, a task list — Trak can read it, analyze it, and tell you what it means.
